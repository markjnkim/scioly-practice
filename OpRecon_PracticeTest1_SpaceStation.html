🔧 Operation: Reconstruction

Practice Test 1 — "The Straw & Cup Space Station"
Division A Grades 4–6 25 min total Woodsbury 2026

🎯 What Is This Event?

Think of it like a game of telephone — but instead of whispered words, you use written instructions to describe how to build something. One teammate (the Writer) sees a mystery object and writes instructions. The other teammate (the Builder) reads those instructions and tries to recreate the object without ever seeing it!

🧠 Memory Trick: Think of this event like giving someone directions to your house. The more specific you are ("turn LEFT at the big red mailbox"), the less likely they'll get lost!

🚫 The Big Rules

  • NO drawings, diagrams, or pictures in the written description — instant Tier 2!
  • NO talking between Writer and Builder during the event.
  • Writer may NOT touch the object.
  • Only words, numbers, single letters, abbreviations, and scientific symbols are allowed.
  • Builder gets extra decoy pieces mixed in — don't use them all!

👁️ The Object: "Straw & Cup Space Station"

Below is the object the Writer sees. Study it carefully and write instructions for the Builder. Remember: NO drawings!

🔍 Object Description (for Event Supervisor / Coach)

Assembly Summary:

  1. Base: 1 small paper cup turned upside-down (open end facing down) sitting on the table. Place a red dot sticker on the center of the front face of the cup.
  2. Vertical Straw: 1 yellow straw inserted vertically through the center of the cup bottom (now facing up), sticking up about 10 cm.
  3. Top Ball: 1 small Styrofoam ball pushed onto the top of the vertical straw. Place a blue dot sticker on the upper-right side of the ball.
  4. Push Pins in Ball: Insert 1 red push pin into the very top of the ball (pointing straight up). Insert 1 blue push pin into the left side of the ball (pointing left and slightly up).
  5. Side Straws (Antennae): Insert 1 straw horizontally into the left side of the cup (about 1 cm from the top/bottom of inverted cup), sticking out about 8 cm. Insert 1 straw horizontally into the right side at the same height.
  6. Pipe Cleaner Rings: Bend 1 red pipe cleaner into a small oval ring and attach it to the outer end of the LEFT straw. Bend 1 blue pipe cleaner into a small oval ring and attach it to the outer end of the RIGHT straw.
  7. Landing Legs: Tape or insert 2 popsicle sticks under the rim of the upside-down cup at an outward angle (~20°) to form landing legs — 1 on the left side and 1 on the right side.
✏️ Writer's Challenge: Write detailed step-by-step instructions for building this "Space Station" using only words and numbers. Be specific about colors, directions (left/right/up/down), quantities, and how pieces connect!

💡 Writer Super-Strategies

  1. Inventory first! Before writing ANY instructions, count and list every single piece (type, color, size). This takes ~1 minute but saves confusion later. Think of it like a recipe — list ingredients first!
  2. Use compass directions — agree with your partner on a system: "front" = the side facing you, "left" = your left. Or use clock positions: "Place the pin at the 12 o'clock position."
  3. Build from the ground UP. Always start with the base/bottom piece first and add from there. Think of it like building a house — foundation first, roof last!
  4. Be a robot. Pretend you're programming a robot. Robots don't understand "kind of near the top" — they need "exactly 2 cm from the top edge, centered left-to-right."
  5. Name every piece! Give pieces simple names: "Cup 1," "Straw A," "Red Pin." Then use those names consistently throughout your instructions.
  6. Describe connections precisely. "Straw goes in cup" is vague. "Insert Straw A vertically through the center of the cup bottom, pushing it down about 3 cm" is clear!

🔨 Builder Super-Strategies

  1. Read the WHOLE thing first! Don't start building the moment you get the paper. Read it all the way through (takes ~1 minute). You'll understand the big picture and avoid ripping apart mistakes.
  2. Sort your materials into groups before building — put all straws together, all pins together, etc. This makes finding the right piece faster.
  3. Check off each step as you complete it. Use a pencil to put a tiny check mark next to each instruction you've finished.
  4. If something is confusing, skip it and come back. Don't waste 3 minutes on one step — build what you CAN and return to tricky parts.

🤝 Team Practice Drills

🏠 Drill 1 — Kitchen Describe: One partner builds a small structure from household items (spoons, cups, rubber bands). The other writes instructions just by looking. Then swap!
🧱 Drill 2 — LEGO Challenge: Build a 10-15 piece LEGO structure. Practice using a grid system (row, column) to describe where each brick goes. Example: "Place the 2×4 red brick so its left corner is at position row-1, column-3 of the green baseplate."
⏰ Drill 3 — Speed Round: Set a 5-minute timer for writing. This forces you to be concise and only include the most important details. If you can nail it in 5 minutes, 10 minutes will feel like luxury!
🗣️ Drill 4 — Create Your Vocabulary: Before competition day, agree on shorthand terms with your partner. For example: "H" = horizontal, "V" = vertical, "CW" = clockwise, "⟶" = pointing right. Abbreviations ARE allowed!
