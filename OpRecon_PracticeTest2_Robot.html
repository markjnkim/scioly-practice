🤖 Operation: Reconstruction

Practice Test 2 — "The Mixed-Material Robot"
Division A Grades 4–6 25 min total Difficulty: Medium-Hard

🤖 Why This Test Is Harder

This practice build uses a mix of different material types (LEGO-style bricks, craft materials, and household items) which is exactly what real tournament objects look like. The tricky parts are:

⚡ Challenge 1: Multiple colors of the same piece type — you must specify which color goes where!
⚡ Challenge 2: Some pieces are INSIDE or BEHIND other pieces — describing hidden or layered parts is tough!
⚡ Challenge 3: Left-right mirror images — make sure your Builder knows YOUR left from THEIR left!

👁️ The Object: "Mixed-Material Robot"

The Writer sees this object. Study every detail — colors, positions, angles, connections!

🔍 Assembly Guide (Coach / Supervisor Reference)

  1. Feet: Lay 2 popsicle sticks (A & B) flat and parallel on the table, about 10 cm apart, horizontal.
  2. Legs: Stand 2 popsicle sticks (C & D) vertically — Stick C on center of Stick A, Stick D on center of Stick B. Secure with tape at base.
  3. Body: Place 1 red solo cup upside-down (opening facing down) on top of the two vertical leg sticks, so the sticks support it from below. Tricky
  4. Rubber Band: Stretch 1 rubber band around the middle of the cup horizontally (like a belt).
  5. Belly Sponge: Tuck the green sponge under the rubber band on the front of the cup, lower half.
  6. Buttons: Insert 3 toothpicks vertically into the sponge in a vertical line (evenly spaced, top to bottom).
  7. Arms: Insert 1 green pipe cleaner into each side of the cup near the top. Bend each at a 90° angle downward about 5 cm out (like elbows). Hard
  8. Left Hand: Curl the bottom of the left arm pipe cleaner around a ping pong ball (Ball 2) so the robot "holds" it.
  9. Head: Place 1 ping pong ball (Ball 1) on top of the cup (the flat bottom of the inverted cup).
  10. Eyes: Stick 2 googly eyes on the front of Ball 1, side by side, in the upper half.
  11. Nose: Glue or press the red pom-pom below the eyes on Ball 1.
  12. Hat: Fold the index card in half diagonally to make a triangle "hat." Place it on top of Ball 1. Tricky
  13. Antenna: Insert the yellow pipe cleaner through the top point of the hat, curling the top into a small spiral/ball shape.

🚀 Advanced Writer Techniques

The "3-Section Method" Tournament Level

Pro writers break their instructions into three clear sections. Think of it like a recipe book:

  1. SECTION 1 — Materials Inventory (1–2 min): List every piece used, including color. Example: "You will use: 1 red cup, 2 green pipe cleaners, 1 yellow pipe cleaner, 4 popsicle sticks, 1 green sponge, 3 toothpicks, 2 ping pong balls, 2 googly eyes, 1 red pom-pom, 1 index card, 1 rubber band." This tells your Builder which pieces are decoys immediately!
  2. SECTION 2 — Build Steps (6–7 min): Numbered step-by-step instructions, building from the BOTTOM UP. Each step references named pieces from Section 1.
  3. SECTION 3 — Final Check (1 min): A quick summary checklist: "When done, the robot should have: 2 feet flat on table, 2 legs, 1 upside-down red cup body, arms with elbows, a face with 2 eyes and red nose, a triangle hat with yellow curly antenna, and the left hand holding a ball."

🧭 The Compass System

Before competition, agree with your partner on a direction system. The best one for elementary level:

TermMeansExample
FrontThe side facing the Builder when they look at it straight on"Stick the sponge on the FRONT of the cup"
BackThe side facing away from the Builder"No pieces go on the BACK"
Left / RightFrom the object's perspective (like a person facing you)"The robot's LEFT arm (your right when facing it)"
Top / BottomUp = toward ceiling, Down = toward table"Place the ball on TOP of the cup"
Inside / OutsideInside the cup vs. outside surface"The rubber band goes OUTSIDE the cup"
🪞 Mirror Trick: When the Writer writes "left arm," they should add "(your right side when facing the robot)" — this prevents the most common mistake in the event!

🏠 Practice Builds to Do at Home

🍽️ Kitchen Robot: Stack a can on a box, add fork arms, a ball of foil for a head, and a paper hat. Practice writing + building!
🧊 Block Tower: Use 8–12 LEGO bricks in 3+ colors. Build a small sculpture. Partner writes, you build from the description.
🧸 Stuffed Animal Dress-Up: Put a hat, scarf, and sunglasses on a stuffed animal. Partner describes placement; you recreate on a different stuffed animal.
📐 Measurement Practice: Use a ruler during practice to describe exact sizes: "The straw sticks out 7 cm to the left." At the real tournament, you can estimate if rulers aren't handy.
